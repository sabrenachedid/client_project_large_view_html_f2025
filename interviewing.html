Overview of Interviews

What is a Typical Interview?

  • 30-45 minute pitch to demonstrate you can do the job and show the employer the value add you’d bring to the company!
  • Opportunity for you to assess whether or not you’d want to work at the organization/with these people/in this role

What You Need to be Aware Of

  • You need to know what you want
  • Confident/assertive behavior is valued by U.S. employers (attire, eye contact and body language are all important)
  • Decisions are made early!
  • Interviewers may have biases they bring to the interview

Types of Interviews

In addition to in-person interviews, there are three common remote interview formats being used:

  • Live virtual interviews more closely mirror an in-person interview with a live interviewer (in real time) on the other side of the screen. Check out this advice on mailing the live virtual interview.
  • Pre-recorded interviews (also called “one-sided”) are when an interviewer isn’t present and you record and submit your answers to interview questions. Here’s a quick video about 4 Strategies for Nailing the (Pre-Recorded) Video Interview.
  • Telephone Ainterviews are still popular, especially as a first step in the interview screening process. Read through these tips on successful phone interviews.

STAR Technique

(Prepare) Listen to the question and think of an event.

  • Situation: Describe the event or situation that you were in
  • Task: Explain the task you had to complete
  • Action: Describe the actions you took to complete the task.
  • Result: Close with the result of your efforts
